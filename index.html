Amazon Review Modeling

What actually separates a 5-star review from a 1-star one? I analyzed the full Amazon product review dataset to find out, using word-frequency analysis to surface the language patterns that distinguish glowing reviews from scathing ones, then building logistic regression and random forest models to classify review sentiment. The first pass ran on a 10,000-review sample; the final version scales to the complete multi-million-record dataset, using streaming techniques to process data that's too large to load into memory at once.

Does Sugar Drive Cereal Popularity?

Tested whether sugar content predicts a cereal's consumer rating and shelf placement using the classic 80 Cereals dataset. A Welch two-sample t-test found shelf 2 (eye-level) cereals carry significantly less sugar than shelf 3, yet rank higher with consumers, overturning the original hypothesis that more sugar means more popular.

Optimizing Mexican Rice with a Factorial Experiment

Designed and ran a 2³ full factorial experiment testing how garlic, chicken broth, and tomato sauce levels affect the taste and texture of Mexican rice, scored by a panel of taste testers. Built a full regression model with all main effects and interactions, then used Lenth's method and a half-normal plot to identify which factors actually moved the needle, finding chicken broth had the most practically meaningful effect, with the optimal recipe predicted to score 9.0/10.

Clutch Rebound Differential

Built a new basketball metric, Clutch Rebound Differential per 100 possessions (CRD/100), to measure whether rebounding in the final five minutes of close games (margin ≤ 5 points) actually predicts winning. Using 2024 NBA play-by-play data, found a moderate positive correlation between CRD/100 and win rate: teams in the top 12% of clutch rebounders won 70% of those games, versus just 30% for teams in the bottom 12%.

Reseeding MLB Playoffs

Investigated whether MLB's playoff seeding system, which guarantees division winners a top-3 seed regardless of regular season record, actually rewards the best teams. Using the 2025 season as a case study (the Yankees tied Toronto for the AL's best record but were seeded 4th on a tiebreaker, then lost to the division-winning Blue Jays in the next round), proposed and modeled an alternative format: seed strictly by record, then re-pair matchups after each round so the top remaining seed always faces the weakest.
